Office of Financial Affairs

The Office of Financial Affairs plays a pivotal role in safeguarding the financial health of the University. With a steadfast commitment to excellence, we ensure revenues and expenses are meticulously balanced and that financial reports adhere to the highest accounting standards. Through diligent oversight of financial disbursements and the implementation of robust policies, we uphold accuracy and compliance at every level. Our dedication to maintaining impeccable financial records reflects our unwavering focus on supporting the University’s mission with integrity and precision."

  • Bursar

    The mission of the Office of the Bursar is to manage:

    • Student accounts including collection of student tuition and fees; student tuition and fee installments program. Management, billing and collection of student long and short-term accounts receivable.
    • Investment functions include managing investable cash assets to optimize return and liquidity in compliance with pertinent federal, state and University of Texas System investment policies.
    • Cashier functions include serving as the depository for institutional funds and administering the institution's petty cash fund.
    • Cash management functions include maintenance of adequate liquidity to fund short term cash needs and management and coordination of Health Science Center banking services.

    Annual Audit Report

    The Texas Internal Auditing Act requires state agencies and institutions of higher education to file an annual report on internal audit activity and the individual internal audit reports. The annual report is submitted to the State Auditor's Office, the Office of the Governor, the Legislative Budget Board, and the Sunset Advisory Commission. (.pdf format)

    Annual Financial Report-Primary Statements

    In accordance with Article IX, Part 7 of the General Appropriations Act, no later than November 20th of each year shall the executive head of each state agency file with the Governor's Office, the Legislative Budget Board, and the Legislative Reference Library an annual report as of August 31st of the preceding fiscal year showing the use of appropriated funds. An annual report shall be prepared by the agency to present the financial position and the results of its operations and changes in financial position for the fiscal year in conformity with reporting guidance provided by the Texas Comptroller of Public Accounts.   • Property Control

    The Office of Property Control assists departments in the safeguarding of institutional furniture and equipment and providing appropriate tagging and timely accounting for such assets in accordance with State of Texas and institutional regulations and policies. The Office of Property Control oversees the following: Development of property control policies and procedures.

    • Administration of final disposition of surplus, salvage, damaged, trade-ins, cannibalized, transfers, donations, obsolete, lost and stolen property, etc.
    • Administration of the annual physical inventory of the institutional equipment.
    • Ongoing investigations and searches for missing property.
    • Maintenance of inventory records.
    • Reporting of information to the State of Texas Statewide Property Accounting System (SPA).
    • State Purchasing
    • eXpendit State Purchasing Policies

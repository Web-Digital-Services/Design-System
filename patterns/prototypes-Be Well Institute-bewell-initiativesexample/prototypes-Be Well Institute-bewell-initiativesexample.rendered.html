Accelerating Adoption of Medication-Based Treatment for Stimulant Use Disorder

Although stimulant use disorder (StUD) is on the rise in the US, fewer than one-third of adults receive treatment and there are no FDA-approved medication-based treatments, despite evidence of clinical efficacy.

Substance use treatment occurs within a complex landscape, with implementation requiring coordination between diverse partners and careful consideration of individual, organizational, and societal-level influences.

To accelerate implementation, our team worked on development of a partner- and theory-driven multi-component implementation strategy to support widespread adoption of medications for StUD.
